AMA Case Competition — Singapore Airlines

Role: Strategy & slides · Year: 2024 · Tags: Marketing, Strategy, Presentation

Marketing Strategy KPI framework

Context

Undergraduate case competition focused on Singapore Airlines. Our team was asked to propose a multi-channel marketing plan that could drive growth over a five-year horizon while staying aligned with brand positioning and financial constraints.

We needed a structured launch plan with clear KPIs, realistic assumptions, and a narrative that could be delivered in a short live presentation to judges.

My contribution

  • Synthesized market and competitor research into a concise strategy direction.
  • Built a KPI framework and five-year outlook with basic financial projections and scenario checks.
  • Authored the executive slides and presented the recommendation to judges.

Process

  • Research: Collected secondary data on routes, customer segments, and competitor campaigns.
  • Synthesis: Narrowed options to a focused set of initiatives with clear audiences and channels.
  • Strategy & design: Translated the plan into a phased roadmap, KPI table, and financial snapshot.
  • Validation & delivery: Checked feasibility against budget and timing; rehearsed and delivered the pitch.

Result & reflection

Produced a cohesive slide deck and live presentation that tied goals, tactics, and KPIs together. Even without formal competition metrics, the project strengthened my ability to scope assumptions and communicate trade-offs clearly.

Next time I would deepen the sensitivity analysis on key metrics and bring in more visual storytelling for the financials.

EN↔JP Technical Localization — Derived Samples

Role: Translator & reviewer · Tags: Localization, Regulated content, JP·EN·ZH

Localization Clinical/technical Japanese

Context

To demonstrate readiness for internship-level localization work, I created paraphrased translation samples that mimic clinical and technical documents without using proprietary material.

The goal was to balance accuracy, naturalness, and consistency while aligning closely with terminology used in Japanese medical and scientific communication.

My contribution

  • Produced paraphrased JP translations imitating clinical and technical content.
  • Created a mini glossary and style notes to keep terminology consistent.
  • Documented translation choices and edge cases for future reference.

Process

  • Research: Reviewed reference materials and official terminology sources in Japanese.
  • Synthesis: Identified recurring patterns in how technical concepts are conveyed.
  • Localization: Drafted EN↔JP samples, refining tone and register to sound natural yet precise.
  • Validation: Re-read for consistency and alignment with the glossary, adjusting ambiguous phrases.

Result & reflection

Built a small but representative set of samples that show how I handle technical concepts in Japanese while preserving meaning from the English. The work also clarified which resources and checks are most useful when working under time pressure.

Going forward, I would add more examples that mix narrative explanations with numeric data and tables.

King County Market Analysis — Real-Estate Brief

Role: Lead analyst · Tags: Research, Analytics, Real estate

Research Analytics Brief

Context

Course project framing a hypothetical $100M investment in the King County / Bellevue area. We were asked to assess market conditions, identify risks, and recommend a direction with support from public data and industry commentary.

The deliverable was a concise written brief aimed at stakeholders who needed a clear, risk-aware recommendation.

My contribution

  • Collected public records, broker reports, and news sources on supply, demand, and pricing.
  • Modeled simple demand/supply scenarios and highlighted key risk factors.
  • Drafted a risk-adjusted recommendation with citations and rationale.

Process

  • Research: Compiled data on vacancy rates, pipeline, and demographic trends.
  • Synthesis: Grouped risks into categories (market, financial, operational) and estimated impact.
  • Strategy: Positioned a recommended asset type and timing based on those scenarios.
  • Delivery: Wrote a brief that highlighted assumptions, risks, and next steps for due diligence.

Result & reflection

The memo presented a structured, evidence-backed recommendation and made uncertainties explicit instead of hiding them. It also showed how non-technical audiences can still be walked through data-driven reasoning.

Next time I would add more visuals, such as a simple risk matrix and scenario comparison charts.

INFO 201 — Data Analysis & Visualization (R + Quarto)

Role: Analyst & author · Tags: Data, Visualization, R, Quarto

Data Visualization R

Context

INFO 201 focused on cleaning, transforming, and visualizing public datasets in R, then communicating findings through Quarto reports that remain reproducible.

The goal was not advanced modeling, but clear data pipelines, labeled charts, and transparent assumptions.

My contribution

  • Cleaned data with dplyr and related R packages.
  • Created charts in ggplot2 with clear labels and captions.
  • Authored the Quarto narrative and ensured the report was reproducible.

Process

  • Data cleaning: Tidied raw data, handled missing values, and created derived variables.
  • Visualization: Designed charts that matched the questions being asked instead of overcomplicating them.
  • Communication: Wrote narrative text that avoided causal claims and explained limitations.
  • Reproducibility: Organized code chunks and parameters so the report can be re-run on demand.

Result & reflection

The report provided a simple but transparent data story. It also made me more comfortable using R as a supporting tool for communication, not as an identity.

Going forward, I would focus on choosing fewer, more impactful charts and adding short summaries for non-technical readers.

UWiB Marketing — Social Graphics

Role: Designer & coordinator · Tags: Design, Content, Student org

Design Content Community

Context

As part of University of Washington Women in Business (UWiB), I helped create event marketing materials that needed to be concise, on-brand, and easy for busy students to understand at a glance.

The work included Instagram posts, story graphics, and simple posters, along with tracking opportunities and deadlines.

My contribution

  • Designed event flyers and short social posts aligned to UWiB’s tone and guidelines.
  • Maintained a deadlines and opportunities tracker to keep content on schedule.
  • Adjusted copy and layout to keep information clear in small spaces.

Process

  • Briefing: Confirmed event details, target audience, and key message with organizers.
  • Design: Built layouts in Canva that prioritized clarity and scannability.
  • Iteration: Incorporated feedback and made quick adjustments to timing, wording, or emphasis.
  • Coordination: Logged posts and deadlines so nothing was missed.

Result & reflection

Produced a set of simple, readable graphics that supported UWiB events and highlighted opportunities for members.

In future work, I would like to experiment more with accessibility checks (contrast, alt text, and motion preferences).
