AMA Case Competition — Singapore Airlines

Role: Strategy & slides · Year: 2024

Marketing Strategy KPI framework

Context

Undergraduate case competition focused on Singapore Airlines. Our team was asked to propose a multi-channel marketing plan over five years, aligned with brand positioning and financial constraints.

My contribution

  • Synthesized market and competitor research into a concise strategy direction.
  • Built a KPI framework and five-year outlook with basic financial projections.
  • Authored executive slides and presented the recommendation to judges.

Process

  • Research: Collected secondary data on routes, customer segments, and competitor campaigns.
  • Synthesis: Narrowed options to a focused set of initiatives with clear audiences and channels.
  • Strategy & design: Translated the plan into a phased roadmap, KPI table, and financial snapshot.

Result & reflection

Produced a cohesive deck tying goals, tactics, and KPIs together. The project strengthened my ability to scope assumptions and communicate trade-offs clearly.

EN↔JP Technical Localization — Derived Samples

Role: Translator & reviewer

Localization Clinical/technical JP·EN

Context

To demonstrate readiness for internship-level localization work, I created paraphrased translation samples that mimic clinical and technical documents without using proprietary material.

My contribution

  • Produced paraphrased JP translations imitating clinical and technical content.
  • Created a mini glossary and style notes for consistent terminology.
  • Documented translation choices and edge cases for future reference.

Process

  • Research: Reviewed reference materials and official terminology sources in Japanese.
  • Synthesis: Identified patterns in how technical concepts are conveyed.
  • Localization: Drafted EN↔JP samples, refining tone and register to be natural yet precise.

Result & reflection

Built a small but representative set of samples that show how I handle technical concepts in Japanese while preserving meaning from English.

King County Market Analysis — Real-Estate Brief

Role: Lead analyst

Research Analytics Real estate

Context

Course project framing a hypothetical $100M investment in the King County / Bellevue area, assessing market conditions, risks, and a recommended direction using public data and commentary.

My contribution

  • Collected public records, broker reports, and news sources on supply, demand, and pricing.
  • Modeled simple demand/supply scenarios and highlighted key risk factors.
  • Drafted a risk-aware recommendation with citations and rationale.

Process

  • Research: Compiled data on vacancy rates, pipeline, and demographic trends.
  • Synthesis: Grouped risks into categories and estimated relative impact.
  • Strategy: Positioned a recommended asset type and timing based on those scenarios.

Result & reflection

The memo presented a structured, evidence-backed recommendation and made uncertainties explicit instead of hiding them.

INFO 201 — Data Analysis & Visualization (R + Quarto)

Role: Analyst & author

Data Visualization R / Quarto

Context

INFO 201 focused on cleaning, transforming, and visualizing public datasets in R, then communicating findings through reproducible Quarto reports.

My contribution

  • Cleaned data with dplyr and related packages.
  • Created charts in ggplot2 with clear labels and captions.
  • Authored the Quarto narrative and ensured the report was reproducible.

Process

  • Data cleaning: Tidied raw data, handled missing values, and created derived variables.
  • Visualization: Chose charts that matched the questions instead of overcomplicating them.

Result & reflection

The report provided a simple but transparent data story and made me more comfortable using R as a supporting tool for communication.

UWiB Marketing — Social Graphics

Role: Designer & coordinator

Design Content Student org

Context

As part of University of Washington Women in Business (UWiB), I created event marketing materials that needed to be concise, on-brand, and easy for busy students to understand at a glance.

My contribution

  • Designed event flyers and short social posts aligned to UWiB’s tone and guidelines.
