AMA Case Competition — Singapore Airlines

Role: Marketing strategist · Year: 2024
Marketing Strategy Presentation
Duration ~6 weeks
Team size 4
Tools PowerPoint, Excel, Canva

Context

The American Marketing Association case asked teams to design a launch and growth strategy for Singapore Airlines in a competitive market. We needed a realistic plan that balanced brand equity, demand uncertainty, and financial constraints while fitting a student competition scope and judging rubric.

My contribution

  • Synthesized market and competitor research; built KPI framework and five-year outlook; authored executive slides; presented to judges.

Process

  • Research: Collected secondary data on target segments, competitive routes, and partnership benchmarks.
  • Synthesis: Prioritized opportunities and risks; aligned team on positioning and growth levers.
  • Strategy & design: Defined channel mix, KPI ladder, and phased roadmap; structured the briefing deck.
  • Validation: Sanity-checked financial model and storytelling flow with peer feedback before final presentation.
  • Delivery: Presented the strategy and financial outlook to judges with a concise, visual deck.

Result & evidence

Delivered a multi-channel launch plan with a KPI framework and five-year outlook that met the judging criteria and clearly communicated assumptions and trade-offs. The final deck highlighted channel priorities, financial projections, and risk mitigations in a format ready for decision-makers.

Reflection

Next time, I would build a more detailed sensitivity analysis on key assumptions (fuel prices, partnership structure) and incorporate more primary research to validate segment hypotheses.

EN↔JP Technical Localization — Derived Samples

Role: JP·EN translator · Year: 2024
Localization Regulated Content JP·EN
Duration Ongoing samples
Languages Japanese · English
Tools Docs, reference guidelines

Context

I built a set of self-contained EN↔JP samples that imitate clinical and technical content without exposing proprietary materials. The goal was to demonstrate my ability to handle regulated-style language, consistent terminology, and natural Japanese while keeping content safe to share publicly.

My contribution

  • Produced paraphrased JP translations imitating clinical/technical content; created mini glossary; documented choices for consistency.

Process

  • Research: Reviewed public Japanese medical and technical references for terminology and tone.
  • Synthesis: Identified typical sentence patterns, disclaimers, and domain-specific phrasing.
  • Localization: Drafted and refined translations with attention to register, readability, and legal-sounding clarity.
  • Verification: Cross-checked terminology across the mini glossary and sample pages.
  • Packaging: Combined samples into a compact PDF that can be safely shared with recruiters and hiring managers.

Result & evidence

The final samples show internship-level clinical and technical localization work with consistent terminology and professional register. They demonstrate that I can translate, localize, and document decisions in a way that supports review and quality assurance.

Reflection

For future iterations, I would expand the glossary to cover more edge cases and add side-by-side commentary to show how I resolve ambiguous phrases.

INFO 201 — Data Analysis & Visualization (R + Quarto)

Role: Data analyst · Year: 2024
Data Visualization Quarto
Duration Academic quarter
Tools R, dplyr, ggplot2, Quarto

Context

INFO 201 assignments focused on using public datasets to answer social and policy questions. I needed to clean and transform data, build clear charts, and explain findings in a way that non-technical readers could follow.

My contribution

  • Cleaned data with dplyr; created charts in ggplot; authored Quarto narrative; ensured reproducibility.

Process

  • Research: Selected relevant public datasets and clarified the questions to answer.
  • Transformation: Used dplyr to filter, join, and summarize data into analysis-ready tables.
  • Visualization: Built ggplot charts emphasizing comparisons and trends aligned with the narrative.
  • Documentation: Wrote Quarto text blocks explaining methods, assumptions, and limitations.
  • Reproducibility: Kept all code and narrative in one Quarto file so results can be re-run easily.

Result & evidence

Produced reproducible reports that combine R code, charts, and commentary. Each deliverable helps non-technical readers see where the data came from, how it was processed, and what it suggests.

Reflection

In future work, I would add more sensitivity checks and confidence intervals, and experiment with interactive dashboards where appropriate.

King County Market Analysis — Real-Estate Brief

Role: Researcher · Year: 2024
Research Analytics Real Estate
Duration ~3 weeks
Team size Individual brief

Context

This hypothetical brief assumed a $100M allocation for a real-estate investment in King County. I needed to assess submarkets, supply and demand trends, and risk factors to recommend a direction grounded in data.

My contribution

  • Collected public records and broker reports; modeled demand/supply scenarios; drafted risk-adjusted recommendation.

Process

  • Research: Pulled data on vacancies, absorption, new supply, and pricing across submarkets.
  • Synthesis: Identified segments with favorable long-term fundamentals and manageable risks.
  • Scenario modeling: Built simple demand/supply scenarios to stress-test the recommendation.
  • Briefing deck: Structured the recommendation into a concise, citation-backed written brief.

Result & evidence

The brief clearly explained where and why the hypothetical fund should allocate capital, along with key risks and data sources. It demonstrates my ability to combine research with simple, transparent assumptions.

Reflection

If I revisit this project, I would add more sensitivity analysis on cap rates and financing conditions and incorporate maps to visualize submarket differences.
